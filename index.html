🖵 Individual Screens

Each SOC dashboard is designed around a 1920 × 1080 baseline. At Full HD, all grid layouts display without collapsing — the three-column SIEM dashboard, the world map with side panels, the incident timeline, and the threat intel feeds all render at full fidelity.

At 1366 × 768 (typical laptop), the layouts switch to single-column stacking via media queries. Everything remains functional but requires scrolling to see all panels.

Tip: Use F11 for fullscreen mode in any browser to reclaim the address bar and tab space — this makes a noticeable difference on smaller screens.

📺 Multi-Screen Viewer (Quad View)

The multi-screen viewer divides your display into a 2×2 grid of iframes. Each quadrant gets half your width × half your height, so the effective resolution per dashboard is:

1080p display → ~960 × 490 per quadrant (tight — some stacking occurs)
1440p display → ~1280 × 670 per quadrant (comfortable)
4K display → ~1920 × 1030 per quadrant (optimal — each quadrant is effectively Full HD)

For presentations, a 4K display or projector is the ideal choice since each quadrant gets a full 1080p-equivalent viewport.

🏫 Classroom & Lab Setup

The ideal classroom setup depends on viewing distance and budget:

Budget-friendly: A single 55" 1080p TV running one dashboard at a time, cycling through screens. Effective from 6–10 feet viewing distance.

Recommended: A 55–65" 4K TV running the multi-screen viewer in fullscreen. All four dashboards are visible simultaneously with readable text from 6–10 feet.

Best experience: Four separate 24–27" monitors, each running one dashboard. This most closely mirrors a real SOC environment and gives students the immersive experience of scanning across multiple screens.

🔍 Font Scaling & Readability

The dashboards use pixel-based font sizes (typically 11–14px for data, 16–20px for headers). These do not scale with display DPI. On a large low-resolution display (e.g., 65" 1080p TV at 10ft), text will be physically large but may appear soft. On a 27" 4K monitor up close, everything is crisp but physically small.

The sweet spot depends on the combination of resolution, physical screen size, and viewing distance. For classroom settings, a 55–65" 4K TV viewed from 6–10 feet is the ideal range.
