🖥️ Individual Screen (This View)

This dashboard is optimized for 1920×1080 (Full HD) or higher. At this resolution, all panels, charts, and data feeds display at full fidelity without collapsing.

At 1280×720, the layout still works but some elements may wrap or compress. Below 720p, content may be difficult to read.

💡 Practical Tips

Classroom TVs (55”–65”): Use 4K resolution for maximum clarity. At 1080p, text remains readable from 6–10 feet but may appear softer on larger panels.

Desktop Monitors (24”–32”): 1080p is fine for individual screens. For the multi-screen quad view, 1440p or 4K is strongly recommended.

Projectors: Ensure native 1080p or higher. Lower-resolution projectors will cause text to blur, making alert details difficult to read.

Browser Zoom: If text appears too small, use Ctrl/Cmd + to increase zoom. The dashboards respond well to 110–125% zoom on high-resolution displays.

🔤 Font Scaling & Readability

Dashboard fonts are set in pixels (11–14px base). These don’t scale with display DPI. On a large low-resolution display (e.g. 65” 1080p TV at 10ft), text will be physically large but may appear soft. On a 27” 4K monitor up close, everything is crisp but physically small.

The sweet spot depends on the combination of resolution, physical screen size, and viewing distance. For classroom settings, a 55”–65” 4K TV viewed from 6–10 feet is the ideal range.
